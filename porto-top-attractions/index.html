Cooking an Experience: Eclairs of the Douro

Make French éclairs, sip Portuguese wine, and enjoy a relaxed Douro evening.

Cooking an Experience is a personal, hands-on pastry-and-wine session hosted by Anat in Valbom, just minutes from central Porto. Make choux pastry and pastry cream, fill and glaze your own chocolate éclairs, then enjoy them with Portuguese wine, light snacks, and a beautiful view over the Douro.

Wine, culture & choice

WOW Porto

Seven museum experiences in restored wine cellars, plus restaurants, bars, a wine school, shops, and broad views back toward Porto. Choose one museum that matches your interests instead of trying to rush the whole district.

Best forWine-curious visitors, couples, mixed-interest groups

03
Art, architecture & nature

Serralves

A complete cultural escape: contemporary art, Álvaro Siza’s architecture, the pink Art Deco villa, landscaped park, House of Cinema, and Treetop Walk. Give it room; this is much more than a museum stop.

Best forArt lovers, design people, slow afternoons
Plan your Serralves visit →
04
Immersive & playful

NEONIA Porto

A retro-futurist version of Porto spread across 19 interactive rooms, with audiovisual installations, city-inspired replicas, a mirror maze, infinite room, and playful surprises. An energetic choice when the weather turns.

Best forFamilies, teens, photos, rainy weather

Football & local passion

FC Porto Museum & Stadium

Football explains a real part of Porto’s identity. The museum brings the club’s history, trophies, heroes, and city connection to life; pair it with a stadium visit when the schedule allows.

Mix, don’t cram

Three easy ways to build them into your stay

DAY 1

Orient, then bake

Start with a central Porto walking tour, then head to Valbom for the 2.5-hour Eclairs of the Douro evening. Allow time for the short Uber or Bolt ride from central Porto.

DAY 2

Cross the river

Spend time in Gaia and choose one WOW museum. Stay for the skyline, a tasting, or dinner rather than racing back across the bridge.

DAY 3

Follow your personality

Pick Serralves for culture and space, NEONIA for indoor play, or FC Porto when match-day energy and football history win.

Porto attractions FAQ

Which experience should I book first?

Eclairs of the Douro is limited to six guests, so secure your preferred evening first. Timed museum entries can usually fit around it.

Which attraction is best when it rains?

NEONIA, WOW’s museums, and the FC Porto Museum are strong indoor choices. Serralves mixes indoor spaces with a large outdoor park. Check directly with Cooking an Experience if weather may affect your Douro evening.

Which is best with children?

It depends on age and interests. NEONIA is the most immediately playful; FC Porto works brilliantly for football fans; Eclairs of the Douro suits families who enjoy a relaxed, hands-on baking activity.

Can I visit more than one in a day?

Yes, but avoid turning Porto into a checklist. Pair nearby or contrasting experiences and leave time for transport, food, and spontaneous stops.
